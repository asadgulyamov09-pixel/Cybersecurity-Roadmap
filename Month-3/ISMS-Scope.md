ISMS Scope

Overview

The ISMS Scope defines the boundaries of the Information Security Management System (ISMS).

It identifies which people, locations, systems, information, and business processes are included within the organization's information security management program.

Clearly defining the scope is one of the first steps in implementing ISO/IEC 27001.

---

Purpose

The purpose of the ISMS Scope is to clearly define what the organization is protecting and managing under its Information Security Management System.

A well-defined scope helps ensure consistency during implementation, audits, and continuous improvement.

---

Example Scope

Organization:

SaaS Company

Included within the ISMS Scope:

People

- Employees
- Contractors

Locations

- Head Office

Information Assets

- Customer Database
- Internal Documentation
- Source Code

Technology

- AWS Cloud Infrastructure
- GitHub Repository
- Company Laptops

Business Processes

- Software Development
- IT Operations
- Customer Support

---

Why Scope Is Important

Without a clearly defined scope:

- Responsibilities become unclear.
- Security controls may be applied inconsistently.
- Audits become more difficult.
- Important assets may be overlooked.

A defined scope ensures that everyone understands which assets, people, and processes are protected by the ISMS.

---

Main Lesson

The ISMS Scope defines the boundaries of the Information Security Management System.

Before an organization can manage information security effectively, it must first define what is included within the system.
