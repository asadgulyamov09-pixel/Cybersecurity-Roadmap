NIST Assessments

Overview

A NIST Assessment is used to evaluate how well an organization implements cybersecurity practices and manages risk.

The goal is not to find a perfect organization.

The goal is to identify strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities for improvement.

---

What Is an Assessment?

An assessment is a structured review of cybersecurity controls, processes, and governance practices.

Organizations are evaluated against the NIST Cybersecurity Framework.

Key questions include:

- Are controls implemented?
- Are controls effective?
- Who owns the controls?
- What gaps exist?

Example Assessment

Organization Status:

- MFA = Yes
- Logs = No
- Backups = Yes
- Incident Response Plan = No

Strengths

- MFA implemented
- Backups available

Weaknesses

- No logging
- No Incident Response Plan

Recommendations

Priority 1:

Implement logging and monitoring.

Priority 2:

Develop an Incident Response Plan.

---

Why Assessments Matter

Assessments help organizations:

- Identify security gaps
- Improve risk management
- Strengthen governance
- Prepare for audits
- Improve cybersecurity maturity

---

Main Lesson

Assessments are used to understand:

- What works well
- What is missing
- What should be improved next

Good cybersecurity starts with understanding the current state and continuously improving it.
