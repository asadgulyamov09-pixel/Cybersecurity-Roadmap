# Policy Ownership and Risk Ownership

## Overview

Effective governance requires clear ownership.

Organizations must assign owners to both policies and risks.

Without ownership, accountability becomes unclear and governance processes become ineffective.

---

# Policy Owner

A Policy Owner is responsible for a policy document.

The Policy Owner ensures that the policy:

* Remains current
* Is reviewed regularly
* Reflects business requirements
* Supports security objectives

Typical Policy Owners:

* CISO
* Security Manager
* Governance Manager

Example:

Password Policy

Policy Owner:

CISO

---

# Policy Owner Responsibilities

* Maintain the policy
* Review the policy
* Update the policy
* Approve policy changes

The Policy Owner owns the document.

---

# Risk Owner

A Risk Owner is responsible for managing a specific risk.

The Risk Owner ensures that risks are:

* Identified
* Assessed
* Monitored
* Treated appropriately

Typical Risk Owners:

* IT Manager
* System Owner
* Business Owner
* Department Manager

---

# Risk Owner Responsibilities

* Evaluate risk levels
* Select risk treatments
* Monitor risk status
* Accept or reduce risk

The Risk Owner owns the risk.

---

# Example

Policy:

Access Control Policy

Policy Owner:

CISO

---

Risk:

Unauthorized Access to Systems

Risk Owner:

IT Operations Manager

---

# Key Difference

Policy Owner:

Owns the document.

Risk Owner:

Owns the risk.

Both roles are important for effective governance and cybersecurity management.

---

# Main Lesson

Governance works best when ownership is clearly assigned.

Policy Owner = Responsible for the policy.

Risk Owner = Responsible for the risk.
