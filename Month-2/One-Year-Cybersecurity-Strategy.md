One-Year Cybersecurity Strategy

Overview

A One-Year Cybersecurity Strategy defines how an organization will improve its cybersecurity program over the next 12 months.

The strategy aligns security initiatives with business objectives and provides measurable goals.

---

Vision

Build a resilient cybersecurity program that protects critical assets while supporting business growth.

---

Current State

Current security gaps:

- No Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- No Backup Program
- No Incident Response Plan
- Limited Security Awareness Training

---

Strategic Objectives

1. Implement MFA for all employees.

2. Develop and test a Backup and Recovery Program.

3. Create and implement an Incident Response Plan.

4. Improve employee cybersecurity awareness.

---

Roadmap

Quarter 1

- Implement MFA

Quarter 2

- Deploy and test backup solutions

Quarter 3

- Develop and test the Incident Response Plan

Quarter 4

- Expand Security Awareness Training

---

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- 100% of employees use Multi-Factor Authentication.

- 100% of critical systems have tested and verified backups.

- 100% of employees complete annual cybersecurity awareness training.

---

Success Criteria

The strategy is considered successful when all objectives are completed and the defined KPIs are achieved.

---

Main Lesson

A cybersecurity strategy becomes effective when it includes:

- A clear vision
- Measurable objectives
- A realistic roadmap
- Meaningful KPIs

Together, these elements transform strategy into measurable business results.
