# Governance Committees

## Overview

Governance committees help organizations make informed decisions about security, risk, compliance, and business operations.

They provide oversight, coordination, and accountability across different departments.

---

# What Is a Governance Committee?

A governance committee is a group of stakeholders who meet regularly to discuss important topics and make decisions.

Governance committees help ensure that risks, controls, and business objectives are aligned.

---

# Security Committee

A Security Committee focuses on information security and cyber risk.

Typical members:

* CIO
* CISO
* IT Manager
* Security Manager
* Risk Manager

Responsibilities:

* review security risks;
* discuss incidents;
* approve security initiatives;
* monitor security controls.

---

# Risk Committee

A Risk Committee focuses on enterprise risk management.

Responsibilities:

* review significant risks;
* monitor risk exposure;
* evaluate risk treatment plans;
* support risk-based decision making.

---

# Scenario 1: Multiple Phishing Incidents

## Issue

Several phishing incidents occurred during the month.

## Committee

Risk Committee

## Reason

The committee should review:

* likelihood of future incidents;
* business impact;
* current controls;
* risk treatment options.

Possible controls:

* MFA
* Security Awareness Training
* Email Filtering

---

# Scenario 2: New Cloud Platform

## Risks

* Misconfiguration
* Public Storage Exposure
* Weak IAM Controls
* Data Leakage
* Unauthorized Access

## Committee

Security Committee

## Reason

The committee should review:

* cloud security requirements;
* IAM policies;
* data protection controls;
* compliance requirements.

---

# Benefits of Governance Committees

Governance committees help organizations:

* improve decision-making;
* manage risks effectively;
* align security with business goals;
* strengthen accountability;
* improve communication between teams.

---

# Main Lesson

Strong governance requires collaboration.

Committees help organizations make consistent and risk-informed decisions.
