NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) - Introduction

Overview

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) is one of the most widely used cybersecurity frameworks in the world.

It helps organizations understand, manage, and improve cybersecurity risk.

The framework provides a structured approach to cybersecurity governance and risk management.

---

What is NIST?

NIST stands for:

National Institute of Standards and Technology.

NIST develops standards, guidelines, and best practices that organizations use to strengthen cybersecurity.

---

NIST CSF Functions

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework is built around six core functions.

1. Govern

Focuses on:

- Governance
- Policies
- Risk Management
- Roles and Responsibilities

How do we manage cybersecurity?

2. Identify

Focuses on:

- Assets
- Business Environment
- Risks

What are we protecting?

---

3. Protect

Focuses on:

- MFA
- Access Control
- Security Awareness
- Data Protection

Question:

How do we protect our systems and data?

---

4. Detect

Focuses on:

- Monitoring
- Logging
- Security Alerts

Question:

How do we detect threats?

---

5. Respond

Focuses on:

- Incident Response
- Communication
- Investigation

Question:

How do we respond to incidents?

---

6. Recover

Focuses on:

- Backups
- Disaster Recovery
- Business Continuity

Question:

How do we recover after an incident?

---

Main Lesson

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework helps organizations build and manage cybersecurity programs through six core functions:

Govern → Identify → Protect → Detect → Respond → Recover
