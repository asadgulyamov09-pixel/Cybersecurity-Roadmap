# RACI Matrix

## Overview

The RACI Matrix is a responsibility assignment model used in governance, project management, and cybersecurity programs.

It helps organizations clearly define roles and responsibilities for tasks, controls, and processes.

Without clear accountability, important activities may be delayed, duplicated, or ignored.

---

# What Does RACI Mean?

## Responsible (R)

The person or team that performs the work.

Example:

* IT Team
* Security Analyst

Responsible individuals execute the task.

---

## Accountable (A)

The person who owns the result and has final responsibility.

Example:

* CIO
* CISO
* Department Manager

Only one role should normally be accountable for a specific activity.

---

## Consulted (C)

Individuals or teams that provide expertise, recommendations, or advice.

Examples:

* Security Team
* Legal Team
* Compliance Team

Communication is two-way.

---

## Informed (I)

People who need updates about progress or results.

Examples:

* CEO
* Board of Directors
* Business Owners

Communication is one-way.

---

# Example: Access Control Policy

| Role          | RACI Assignment |
| ------------- | --------------- |
| IT Team       | Responsible     |
| CIO           | Accountable     |
| Security Team | Consulted       |
| CEO           | Informed        |

# Why RACI Is Important

A well-designed RACI Matrix:

* Defines ownership
* Reduces confusion
* Improves accountability
* Prevents duplicated work
* Supports governance processes

Without RACI, teams may not know:

* Who performs the work
* Who approves decisions
* Who should be consulted
* Who needs updates

---

# Common Mistake

Many people confuse:

Responsible ≠ Accountable

Responsible performs the task.

Accountable owns the result.

---

# Main Lesson

Policies define what must be done.

RACI defines who is responsible for making it happen.

Effective governance requires both clear documentation and clear accountability.
