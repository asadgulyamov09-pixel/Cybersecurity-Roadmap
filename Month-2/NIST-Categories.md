NIST CSF Categories

Overview

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) is organized into three levels:

Function → Category → Subcategory

This structure helps organizations understand, assess, and improve their cybersecurity programs.

---

Function

Functions are the highest level of the framework.

The six NIST CSF Functions are:

- Govern
- Identify
- Protect
- Detect
- Respond
- Recover

Functions describe major cybersecurity objectives.

---

Category

Categories divide each Function into specific cybersecurity areas.

Examples:

Govern

Categories:

- Risk Management
- Policies
- Roles and Responsibilities

---

Identify

Categories:

- Asset Management
- Risk Assessment

---

Protect

Categories:

- Identity Management
- Access Control
- Awareness and Training
- Data Security

---

Detect

Categories:

- Continuous Monitoring
- Security Logging

---

Respond

Categories:

- Incident Response
- Communication

---

Recover

Categories:

- Recovery Planning
- Backup Management

---

Subcategory

Subcategories are detailed activities and outcomes.

Examples:

Protect → Access Control

Subcategories:

- MFA enabled
- User authentication implemented
- Access rights reviewed

---

Detect → Monitoring

Subcategories:

- Logs collected
- Security events monitored
- Alerts generated

---

Why Categories Matter

Categories help organizations:

- Assess security maturity
- Identify gaps
- Organize controls
- Improve governance
- Support audits and compliance

---

Examples

Control: MFA

Function:

Protect

Category:

Identity Management and Access Control

---

Control: Security Logs

Function:

Detect

Category:

Security Continuous Monitoring

---

Main Lesson

NIST CSF uses a layered structure:

Function → Category → Subcategory

This allows organizations to move from high-level cybersecurity goals to specific security activities and controls.
