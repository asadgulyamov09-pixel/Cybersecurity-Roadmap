# Governance Structure

## Overview

Governance structure defines how an organization makes decisions, manages risks, implements security controls, and verifies effectiveness.

A strong governance structure helps organizations improve security, reduce risks, and achieve business objectives.

---

# Main Components of Governance Structure

## Board of Directors

The Board provides strategic direction and oversight.

Responsibilities:

* define business objectives;
* approve major decisions;
* establish governance expectations;
* oversee risk management.

Example:

The Board decides that the organization must improve cybersecurity and reduce phishing risks.

---

## Executive Management

Executive Management converts strategic goals into operational actions.

Examples:

* CEO
* CIO
* COO

Responsibilities:

* implement business strategy;
* allocate resources;
* support security initiatives;
* manage projects and operations.

Example:

Management launches a project to implement MFA across the organization.

---

## Risk & Compliance

Risk and Compliance teams help organizations identify, assess, and manage risks.

Responsibilities:

* risk assessment;
* compliance monitoring;
* policy development;
* security governance support.

Example:

The team identifies phishing as a major risk and recommends MFA, security awareness training, and email filtering.

---

## Security Team

Security teams implement technical controls and monitor security operations.

Responsibilities:

* deploy security controls;
* manage security tools;
* monitor threats;
* support incident response.

Example:

The security team configures MFA and deploys email security solutions.

---

## Internal Audit

Internal Audit provides independent assurance.

Responsibilities:

* evaluate controls;
* assess governance effectiveness;
* verify compliance;
* identify improvement opportunities.

Example:

Internal Audit reviews whether MFA has been properly implemented and whether employees are using it.

---

---

# Example: Reducing Phishing Risk

Board:

* requires stronger cybersecurity.

Risk & Compliance:

* identifies phishing risk.
* recommends MFA and awareness training.

Management:

* approves implementation plans.

Security Team:

* deploys MFA and email protection controls.

Internal Audit:

* verifies effectiveness and compliance.

---

# Key Lesson

 - Good cybersecurity depends on clear responsibilities.

- The Board provides direction.

- Management executes plans.

- Risk and Compliance manage risks.

- Security Teams implement controls.

- Internal Audit verifies effectiveness.
