# Roles and Responsibilities

## Overview

Clearly defined roles and responsibilities are essential for effective governance, risk management, and compliance (GRC).

Organizations must know:

* who performs tasks;
* who owns decisions;
* who provides guidance;
* who receives updates.

Without clear responsibilities, risks may be ignored and important controls may fail.

---

# RACI Model

The RACI model helps organizations define responsibilities.

## R – Responsible

The person or team that performs the work.

Example:

* IT Team

---

## A – Accountable

The person who owns the result and has final responsibility.

Example:

* CIO
* Department Manager

Only one role should normally be accountable for a task.

---

## C – Consulted

People or teams that provide expertise and advice.

Examples:

* Security Team
* Legal Team
* Compliance Team

---

## I – Informed

People who are kept informed about progress or results.

Examples:

* CEO
* Board of Directors

# Why Roles Matter

Clearly defined responsibilities help organizations:

* reduce confusion;
* improve accountability;
* manage risks effectively;
* implement controls successfully;
* support governance processes.

---

# Main Lesson

Strong cybersecurity governance requires clear ownership.

Every control, risk, and security process should have a defined owner and responsibility structure.
