NIST CSF Outcomes

Overview

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) focuses on outcomes rather than specific tools.

Organizations are encouraged to achieve security objectives while choosing the controls and technologies that best fit their environment.

---

What Is an Outcome?

An outcome is the security result an organization wants to achieve.

NIST does not require specific software or products.

Instead, it focuses on whether security objectives are being met.

---

NIST CSF Functions and Outcomes

Govern

Purpose:

Manage cybersecurity risk through governance, policies, and oversight.

Example Outcome:

Cybersecurity risks are managed effectively.

---

Identify

Purpose:

Understand assets, systems, data, and risks.

Example Outcome:

Assets and risks are identified.

---

Protect

Purpose:

Implement safeguards to reduce cybersecurity risk.

Examples:

- MFA
- Access Control
- Password Policies

Example Outcome:

Access to systems and data is controlled.

---

Detect

Purpose:

Identify cybersecurity events quickly.

Examples:

- Monitoring
- Logging
- Alerts

Example Outcome:

Threats and incidents are detected in a timely manner.

---

Respond

Purpose:

Take action during an incident.

Examples:

- Incident Response
- Investigation
- Communication

Example Outcome:

Incidents are managed effectively.

---

Recover

Purpose:

Restore operations after an incident.

Examples:

- Backups
- Disaster Recovery
- Business Continuity

Example Outcome:

Business operations are restored successfully.

---

Main Lesson

NIST CSF focuses on outcomes rather than tools.

Function → Outcome

Protect → Access is controlled

Recover → Business operations are restored

Detect → Threats are identified quickly
