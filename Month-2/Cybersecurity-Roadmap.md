Cybersecurity Roadmap

Overview

A Cybersecurity Roadmap is a structured plan that helps an organization achieve its cybersecurity objectives.

The roadmap translates strategy into specific projects and activities over time.

Without a roadmap, organizations often struggle to prioritize initiatives and measure progress.

---

Relationship Between Strategy and Roadmap

Strategy answers:

- Where are we going?

Objectives answer:

- What do we want to achieve?

Roadmap answers:

- How will we get there?

---

Example Roadmap

Organization Issues:

- No MFA
- No Backups
- No Incident Response Plan
- No Security Training

Roadmap:

Q1

Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Purpose:

Reduce unauthorized access risk.

---

Q2

Implement Backup and Recovery Program

Purpose:

Protect against data loss and ransomware.

---

Q3

Develop Incident Response Plan

Purpose:

Improve incident handling capabilities.

---

Q4

Expand Security Awareness Training

Purpose:

Improve employee security behavior.

---

Why Roadmaps Matter

Roadmaps help organizations:

- Prioritize projects
- Allocate resources
- Track progress
- Align security with business goals

---

Common Mistake

Attempting to implement every security initiative at the same time.

This often creates confusion and resource shortages.

A roadmap provides structure and realistic timelines.

---

Main Lesson

Strategy defines direction.

Objectives define targets.

Roadmaps define execution.

Successful cybersecurity programs require all three.
