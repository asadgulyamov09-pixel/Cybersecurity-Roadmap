# Cybersecurity Strategy

## Overview

Cybersecurity Strategy is a long-term plan that helps an organization manage cybersecurity risks while supporting business objectives.

A successful cybersecurity strategy aligns security initiatives with organizational goals.

---

# What Is Cybersecurity Strategy?

Cybersecurity Strategy defines:

* Security priorities
* Risk management approach
* Security investments
* Long-term security objectives

The purpose is to protect the organization while enabling business growth.

---

# Risk Appetite

Risk Appetite is the amount of risk an organization is willing to accept.

Different organizations have different levels of risk appetite.

Examples:

## Bank

Risk Appetite: Low

Reasons:

* Financial data
* Customer information
* Regulatory requirements

---

## Startup

Risk Appetite: High

Reasons:

* Rapid growth
* Innovation
* Limited resources

---

# Business Alignment

Cybersecurity must support business objectives.

Security should not unnecessarily block business operations.

Instead, security should help organizations:

* Grow safely
* Protect customers
* Meet regulatory requirements
* Expand into new markets

---

# Example

Business Goal:

Expand internationally.

Cybersecurity Support:

* Implement ISO 27001
* Improve governance
* Protect customer data
* Meet international compliance requirements
* Secure cloud infrastructure

---

# Why Strategy Matters

Without strategy:

* Security becomes reactive
* Investments become inefficient
* Risks become difficult to manage

With strategy:

* Security supports growth
* Risks are managed effectively
* Resources are used efficiently

---

# Main Lesson

Good cybersecurity supports business goals.

Security exists to enable the business, not prevent it from operating.
