GRC Operating Model

Overview

Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) helps organizations manage risks, implement controls, and achieve business objectives securely.

One of the most important concepts in GRC is understanding the difference between Governance and Management, as well as the Three Lines Model.

---

Governance vs Management

Although these concepts are closely related, they have different responsibilities.

Governance

Governance determines:

- strategic direction;
- policies;
- expectations;
- oversight.

Governance answers the question:

"What should be done?"

Example

An organization decides that Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) must be implemented to improve security.

This decision belongs to Governance.

---

Management

Management is responsible for implementing decisions and operating processes.

Management answers the question:

"How will it be done?"

Example

Management deploys MFA, updates procedures, and ensures employees use the new authentication method.

---

Key Difference

Governance decides.

Management executes.

---

Three Lines Model

The Three Lines Model helps organizations manage risks and responsibilities.

First Line — Operations

The first line consists of business and operational teams.

Examples:

- HR
- Developers
- Finance
- IT Operations

Responsibilities:

- perform daily work;
- manage operational risks;
- follow policies and procedures.

---

Second Line — Risk & Compliance

The second line provides oversight and guidance.

Examples:

- GRC Teams
- Risk Management
- Compliance
- Information Security

Responsibilities:

- develop policies;
- monitor risks;
- support the first line;
- promote compliance.

---

Third Line — Internal Audit

The third line provides independent assurance.

Responsibilities:

- evaluate controls;
- verify compliance;
- assess effectiveness of governance and risk management processes.

Main Lesson

A strong GRC operating model requires clear responsibilities.

Governance provides direction.

Management implements decisions.

Risk and Compliance monitor activities.

Internal Audit independently verifies effectiveness.
