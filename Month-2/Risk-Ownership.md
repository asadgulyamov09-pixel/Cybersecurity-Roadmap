# Risk Ownership

## Overview

Risk ownership is a fundamental concept in Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC).

Every significant risk within an organization should have a clearly defined owner.

A risk owner is responsible for understanding, monitoring, and managing a risk.

---

# What Is a Risk Owner?

A Risk Owner is the individual or team responsible for a specific risk.

The risk owner:

* understands the risk;
* evaluates the risk;
* decides how the risk should be treated;
* ensures appropriate controls are implemented.

A risk owner is not always the person who performs the work.

---

# Risk Treatment Options

Organizations generally have four ways to treat risks.

## Accept

Accept the risk when the impact is low or mitigation costs are higher than the risk itself.

---

## Reduce

Implement controls to decrease the likelihood or impact of a risk.

Examples:

* MFA
* Strong Password Policies
* Security Awareness Training

---

## Transfer

Transfer the risk to another party.

Examples:

* Cyber Insurance
* Outsourcing

---

## Avoid

Avoid the activity that creates the risk.

Example:

* Stop using an insecure system.

---

# Scenario 1: Weak Passwords

## Risk

* Account compromise
* Unauthorized access
* Data leakage

## Possible Risk Owner

* IT Manager
* System Owner

## Treatment

Reduce

Controls:

* MFA
* Strong Password Policy
* Password Manager
* Security Awareness Training

---

# Scenario 2: Public Cloud Storage Bucket

## Risk

* Data leakage
* Unauthorized access
* Sensitive information exposure

## Business Impact

* Customer data exposure
* Reputational damage
* Financial losses
* Regulatory penalties

## Possible Risk Owner

* Cloud Administrator
* Cloud Team Manager
* Data Owner

## Treatment

Reduce

Controls:

* Restrict public access
* Access reviews
* Cloud monitoring
* Least privilege

---

# Main Lesson

Every important risk should have a clearly defined owner.

Without ownership, risks may remain unmanaged and eventually become incidents.
