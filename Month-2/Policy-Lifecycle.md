# Policy Lifecycle

## Overview

Policies are not static documents.

They must be created, reviewed, approved, implemented, monitored, and updated over time.

A strong governance program ensures that policies remain relevant and effective.

---

# Policy Lifecycle Stages

## 1. Draft

The policy is created.

Typically developed by:

* GRC Team
* Security Team
* Governance Team

Example:

Password Policy Draft

---

## 2. Review

The policy is reviewed by stakeholders.

Possible reviewers:

* Security Team
* Legal Team
* Compliance Team

The goal is to identify gaps and improve accuracy.

---

## 3. Approval

The policy receives formal approval.

Typical approvers:

* CIO
* CISO
* Senior Management

Approval makes the policy official.

---

## 4. Implementation

The policy is put into practice.

Example:

The IT Team configures password requirements and MFA settings.

---

## 5. Monitoring

The organization checks whether the policy is being followed.

Examples:

* Access reviews
* Compliance checks
* Security monitoring

---

## 6. Review and Update

Policies must be reviewed regularly.

Updates may be required because of:

* New technologies
* New threats
* New regulations
* Business changes

Example:

Password requirements increase from 8 characters to 12 characters.

---

# Why Policy Updates Matter

Outdated policies may:

* Fail to address new risks
* Ignore new technologies
* Violate current regulations
* Reduce security effectiveness

Regular reviews help maintain strong governance.

---

# Main Lesson

A policy is valuable only when it is maintained.

Draft → Review → Approval → Implementation → Monitoring → Update

This lifecycle ensures policies remain effective and relevant.
