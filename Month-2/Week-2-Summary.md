Week 2 Summary - NIST Cybersecurity Framework

Topics Covered

NIST CSF Overview

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework helps organizations manage cybersecurity risk through a structured approach.

Core Functions:

- Govern
- Identify
- Protect
- Detect
- Respond
- Recover

---

Outcomes

NIST focuses on outcomes rather than specific tools.

Examples:

- Protect → Access is controlled
- Recover → Business operations are restored

---

Categories and Subcategories

Structure:

Function → Category → Subcategory

Examples:

Protect → Access Control → MFA

Detect → Monitoring → Logging

---

Profiles

Current Profile:

Current cybersecurity state.

Target Profile:

Desired cybersecurity state.

Gap Analysis:

Identifies improvements needed to reach the target state.

---

Assessments

Assessments help organizations:

- Identify strengths
- Identify weaknesses
- Prioritize improvements
- Support governance and compliance

---

Cybersecurity Maturity

Maturity Levels:

1. Initial
2. Repeatable
3. Defined
4. Managed
5. Optimized

Higher maturity means stronger and more consistent cybersecurity practices.

---

Key Lessons

- NIST provides a structured cybersecurity framework.
- Security should be measured through outcomes.
- Profiles help identify gaps.
- Assessments reveal strengths and weaknesses.
- Cybersecurity maturity helps organizations improve continuously.
