Governance Documentation

Overview

Documentation is a critical part of Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC).

Security documentation helps organizations define expectations, responsibilities, and controls.

Without documentation, employees may follow different practices, increasing risk and confusion.

---

Policy

A Policy is a high-level statement of management intent.

Example:

Information Security Policy

Purpose:

To protect company information and information systems.

Policies describe what must be achieved.

---

Standard

A Standard defines specific requirements.

Example:

Password Standard

Requirements:

- Minimum 12 characters
- MFA enabled
- Password reuse prohibited

Standards describe mandatory rules.

---

Procedure

A Procedure provides step-by-step instructions.

Example:

How to Enable MFA

1. Open account settings.
2. Select security options.
3. Enable MFA.
4. Verify authentication method.

Procedures describe how tasks are performed.

---

Guideline

Guidelines provide recommendations and best practices.

Example:

Use a password manager to generate strong passwords.

Guidelines are generally optional but recommended.

---

Documentation Hierarchy

Policy
↓
Standard
↓
Procedure
↓
Guideline

---

Why Documentation Matters

Good documentation helps organizations:

- Improve consistency
- Reduce security risks
- Define responsibilities
- Support compliance requirements
- Improve governance effectiveness

---

Main Lesson

Effective cybersecurity governance depends on clear and well-maintained documentation.
