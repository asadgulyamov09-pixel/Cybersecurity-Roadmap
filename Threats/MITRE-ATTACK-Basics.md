# MITRE ATT&CK Basics

## Overview

MITRE ATT&CK is a framework that describes attacker behavior, attack stages, and techniques used during cyber attacks.

It helps security teams understand:
- how attackers operate;
- attack progression;
- defensive gaps;
- business risks.

---

# Tactics vs Techniques

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Tactics | attacker goals |
| Techniques | methods attackers use |

---

# Important MITRE Tactics

## Initial Access

Goal:
- enter the system.

Examples:
- phishing;
- malicious links;
- fake websites.

Business risks:
- unauthorized access;
- compromised accounts.

---

## Credential Access

Goal:
- steal passwords and credentials.

Examples:
- phishing;
- keylogging;
- credential dumping.

Business risks:
- account compromise;
- data leakage.

---

## Persistence

Goal:
- remain inside the system.

Examples:
- startup malware;
- scheduled tasks;
- backdoors.

Business risks:
- long-term unauthorized access;
- hidden attacker activity.

---

## Privilege Escalation

Goal:
- gain higher permissions.

Examples:
- exploiting vulnerabilities;
- abusing admin privileges.

Business risks:
- full system compromise;
- operational disruption.

---

# Attack Flow Example

phishing
↓
stolen credentials
↓
unauthorized login
↓
privilege escalation
↓
data theft

---

# Security Perspective

Cyber attacks often happen in multiple stages.

Understanding attacker behavior helps organizations:

detect attacks earlier;
improve defenses;
reduce business risks.

# Conclusion

MITRE ATT&CK helps cybersecurity professionals understand how attackers move through systems and how technical attacks become business risks.
