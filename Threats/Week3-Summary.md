# Week 3 Summary — Threats & MITRE ATT&CK

## Overview

During Week 3, I focused on cyber threats, attacker behavior, business impact analysis, and risk-based thinking.

This week helped me understand how technical attacks affect businesses, operations, and customer trust.

---

# Topics Covered

## CIA Triad

I learned the three core principles of cybersecurity:
- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

I explored how these principles protect:
- sensitive information;
- system reliability;
- business operations.

---

## Phishing & Credential Theft

I studied:
- email phishing;
- spear phishing;
- fake websites;
- credential theft.

I also learned how phishing attacks can lead to:
- unauthorized access;
- financial loss;
- customer trust issues;
- data leakage.

---

## Ransomware

I explored:
- ransomware attacks;
- encrypted systems;
- operational shutdown;
- backups and recovery.

I learned why ransomware is considered both a technical and business risk.

---

## DDoS & Availability

I studied:
- DoS vs DDoS;
- botnets;
- CDN;
- availability attacks.

I learned how downtime directly impacts organizations financially and operationally.

---

## MITRE ATT&CK

I explored:
- attacker tactics;
- attacker techniques;
- privilege escalation;
- persistence;
- credential access;
- attack stages.

I learned how cyber attacks usually happen step-by-step rather than in a single action.

---

## Risk Mapping

I practiced:
- threat identification;
- likelihood analysis;
- business impact analysis;
- control selection.

I created mini risk registers and threat-to-control mappings.

---

# Main Lessons

Cybersecurity is not only about blocking attacks.

It is also about:
- protecting business continuity;
- reducing risks;
- protecting customer trust;
- understanding attacker behavior.
