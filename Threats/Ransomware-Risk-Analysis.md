# Ransomware Risk Analysis

## Overview

Ransomware is one of the most dangerous cyber threats for modern organizations.

It can:
- encrypt files;
- stop operations;
- make systems unavailable;
- create financial losses;
- damage reputation.

This analysis focuses on the business impact of ransomware attacks and the controls that help reduce risk.

---

# What Is Ransomware

Ransomware is malware that encrypts files or systems and demands payment for recovery.

Attackers may use:
- phishing emails;
- malicious attachments;
- fake websites;
- weak passwords;
- vulnerable systems.

---

# Ransomware Scenario

A company's internal systems become encrypted by ransomware.

Employees cannot access files.
The website becomes unavailable.
Customers cannot place orders.
Attackers demand payment for decryption.

# Business Impacts

Ransomware may cause:
- operational disruption;
- financial losses;
- customer dissatisfaction;
- reputational damage;
- recovery costs;
- downtime.

---

# Controls Against Ransomware

| Control | Purpose |
|---|---|
| backups | recover important data |
| MFA | reduce account compromise |
| awareness training | reduce phishing risk |
| patching | fix vulnerabilities |
| segmentation | limit malware spread |
| monitoring | detect suspicious activity |
| least privilege | reduce attacker access |

---

# Why Backups Matter

Backups are critical because they help organizations:
- recover encrypted data;
- restore operations;
- reduce downtime;
- avoid permanent data loss.

Offline backups are especially important because some ransomware can encrypt online backups.

---

# Incident Response

Basic incident response steps:

1. detect the incident;
2. isolate infected systems;
3. investigate the attack;
4. recover systems from backups;
5. improve security controls.

---

# Threat → Business Impact → Control

| Threat | Business Impact | Control |
|---|---|---|
| ransomware | operational shutdown | backups |
| phishing | credential theft | MFA |
| weak passwords | account compromise | strong password policy |
| DDoS | downtime | DDoS protection |
| data leakage | reputational damage | encryption + access control |

---

# GRC Perspective

Ransomware is not only a technical problem.

It is also a business risk because it affects:
- operations;
- revenue;
- customer trust;
- service availability;
- recovery costs.

---

# Conclusion

Cybersecurity is not only about protecting systems.

It is also about protecting business continuity and keeping operations running during attacks.
